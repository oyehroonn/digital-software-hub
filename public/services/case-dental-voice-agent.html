01 · The brief

Twelve clinics, one phone problem,
and a board that wanted answers.

The group runs general and orthodontic clinics across Dubai, Sharjah, and Abu Dhabi. Each location had a front desk juggling walk-ins, insurance pre-checks, English-Arabic switches, and three booking tools. After-hours calls went to voicemail — and 31% of those callers never called back.

  • Front desks spent 4.5 hours/day on phone tasks that could be templated.
  • Three previous vendors built voice prototypes that failed in real accents and on long pauses.
  • Booking lived in 3 systems. None of them talked to each other.
  • Compliance team needed audit-grade transcripts before anything went live.

"We didn't want another chatbot. We wanted someone our patients could actually talk to — and a system our auditors wouldn't reject."

— Director of Operations · MENA dental group
02 · What we shipped

A bilingual voice agent wired into Cal.com,
with a clinic-aware brain and a paper trail.

A single agent fronts every clinic's main line. It identifies the caller, routes by intent, books or reschedules against each clinic's Cal.com calendar, and sends a confirmation in the patient's preferred language. Every call ships a redacted transcript to the compliance lake.

Why this stack survived production

  • Whisper handles Levantine + Gulf Arabic accents the previous vendor's STT failed on.
  • The agent loop uses tool-calling to validate slot availability before quoting times.
  • 11labs voice cloning lets each clinic keep its existing receptionist's voice for continuity.
  • WORM-style audit table means transcripts can't be retroactively edited — passes compliance.
  • Per-clinic configuration: each location has its own hours, insurance list, and dentist roster.
